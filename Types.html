  • Alphonso Mangoes

    • Available during mid-July, Alphonso Mangoes are internationally loved for their sunshine yellow appearance and delightful taste. Named after Afonso de Albuquerque, this voluptuous king of the mangoes is one of the most consumed varieties of mangoes in India among mango lovers. Ratnagiri and its neighbouring regions in Maharashtra are known to be one of the finest and exclusive hotspots for mango-lovers, widely famous for their Alphonso mangoes.Alphonso mango is a variety of mangoes that is primarily found in India, especially in the western states of Maharashtra and Gujarat. This mango variety is known for its sweet, aromatic, and juicy flesh, vibrant orange color, and creamy texture. It is often referred to as the "King of Mangoes" and is highly sought after by mango enthusiasts all over the world. Alphonso mangoes are typically harvested in the months of April and May and are widely used in desserts, drinks, and other culinary creations. They are also rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, making them a healthy addition to any diet.

    Climate

    Can be grown under both tropical and sub-tropical climate from sea level to 1400 m altitude, provided there is no high humidity, rain or frost during the flowering period. Places with good rainfall and dry summer are ideal for mango cultivation. It is better to avoid areas with winds and cyclones which may cause flower and fruit shedding and breaking of branches.

    Soil

    Mango can be grown on a wide range of soils from alluvial to laterite provided they are deep (minimum 6') and well drained. It prefers slightly acidic soils (pH 5.5 to 7.5)
